## Result and Findings

  The analysis result are summarized below.
  1.  The Revenue Cost Gap: FY Enterprises generated ₦13,039,267,798 in Total Revenue, marking a +4.02% growth compared to 2024. However, the Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) surged faster at +4.66%, hitting ₦10,134,077,992.
  2.  Profit Friction: Because cost growth outpaced revenue growth, Total Profit (₦2,905,189,806) only managed a modest +1.80% YoY increase, squeezing the overall Profit Margin to 22.3%.
  3.  Volume Metrics: Average Order Value (AOV) stood steady at ₦2.61M (+1.47% YoY), supported by 5K total transactions (+2.51% YoY).
  4.  Product Champions: Milk Powder led the company in total revenue generation at ₦1.8B, closely followed by Detergent, Soft Drinks, and Sugar 50kg (each at ₦1.7B).
  5.  Profit Driver: When evaluating pure profit, the Beverages category stood out as the absolute leader, yielding ₦185.47M
  6.  Geographic Hubs: Ibadan, Abuja, and Benin emerged as the top three most profitable cities, each generating approximately ₦0.14B in profit.
  7.  High Risk Revenue: A deep dive into weekend operations highlighted severe cash flow risks. 32.74% (₦260M) of weekend profit sits tied up in Unpaid invoices, and another 31.35% (₦249M) remains trapped in Partial payments.

  ## Recommendation
  Based on the data insights, the following strategic actions are recommended to optimize FY Enterprises' operations and protect future profitability:

  - Address Margin Compression: Address the rising cost of goods by auditing the procurement supply chain. Renegotiate bulk contracts with raw material suppliers or optimize inbound logistics to bring the COGS growth rate below the revenue growth rate.
  - Revamp Weekend Credit & Collection Policies: Implement stricter validation checks on weekend orders to mitigate the 33.36% cancellation rate. Reduce payment risk by introducing automated payment reminders for "Partial" and "Unpaid" balances, or provide small incentives (e.g., a 1% cash discount) for upfront payments.
  - Maximize Beverage and Key Regional Allocations: Double down on the high margin Beverages product lines. Ensure that inventory fulfillment priority is given to the top-performing hubs Ibadan, Abuja, and Benin to capitalize on robust consumer demand and prevent expensive out of stock scenarios.
  - Optimize Pricing Strategies for Top Revenue Drivers: Since essential bulk goods like Milk Powder, Detergent, and Sugar 50kg command high revenue volume but face high production costs, execute minor, data driven price adjustments to shield these core items from inflation.
